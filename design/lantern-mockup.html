"Your word is a lamp to my feet and a light to my path." Psalm 119:105
John 1 · 1–5

1In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God. 2He was with God in the beginning. 3Through him all things were made.

Observation
"Word" is named three ways in one verse. With God, and God. The repetition is deliberate.
Historical
John opens where Genesis does. "In the beginning" is a claim, not a coincidence.
Personal
If he was there at the start, he predates everything I carried into this chapter.
Four lenses

One passage, seen four ways.

Every note you write is one of four kinds. Over time a passage fills in from every angle, and you can read it back lens by lens.

Observation
What the text actually says. Words, structure, repetition, what is there before you interpret it.
Historical
The world behind it. Who wrote it, to whom, and what a first reader would have heard.
Application
What it asks of you. The step from the page into an ordinary week.
Personal
What it stirs in you. A prayer, a doubt, a line worth remembering.
